UI Research Project

by Tingjia Yu

Inter-symmetric

Orb.Farm

Information design

Orb.Farm uses a minimalist approach to information design. There is no traditional homepage or menu, but the layout is straightforward and easy to follow. As soon as users enter the site, they can clearly understand how to begin. The icons are consistent, and the way of interacting—similar to drawing—adds to the fun and usability of the experience. Although the overall interface is user-friendly, some functions lack proper guidance. For example, the environmental indicator panel in the top right corner has no prompt or explanation, which makes it easy to overlook. As a result, users might miss out on more advanced features like adjusting oxygen levels by planting different kinds of plants.

Intersymmetric also has no written explanations and uses an even more minimalist structure. However, its information layout feels somewhat disorganized. The page has no clear title or navigation system, so first-time users may find it difficult to understand the purpose of the site. After clicking any of the three entries, users are taken to a separate loading page, which feels unnecessary and repetitive. The size and layout of the loading buttons are also inconsistent, which affects the overall readability and sense of professionalism.

Although both websites do not include traditional menus or text descriptions, the way they present information is noticeably different. Orb.Farm uses a combination of images and icons to help users quickly understand the page's functions and how to interact with them. The interface delivers information in a clear and direct way. In contrast, Intersymmetric only presents three abstract title links on the homepage, without background context or visual guidance. As a result, users may feel confused and unsure of the site's purpose during their first visit.

Mapping

On the Orb.Farm website, the design of each control clearly expresses its intended function. Each button is labeled with the name of a specific creature, and when clicked, the selected element appears right away. This matches user expectations very well. The placement of creatures is also very straightforward—the creature appears exactly where the user clicks. Users can understand and operate the system without the need for any instructions or documentation.

The interaction in Intersymmetric mainly takes place within the instrument interface. Although users can click or drag elements to control sounds or visuals, the controls themselves offer no labels or hints. As a result, the connection between user actions and system responses is not always clear.

Orb.Farm focuses on clarity in its mapping design, allowing users to easily understand the logic of the controls. Intersymmetric, on the other hand, emphasizes freedom of exploration but sacrifices predictability and guidance. This may lead to confusion, especially for users who are visiting the site for the first time.

Characterisation

Orb.Farm uses a consistent pixel art style combined with soft color tones. Even though it features a wide range of colors, the overall saturation is well controlled, helping to prevent visual fatigue. This creates a calm and comfortable interface environment that supports long-term observation. The icons, animations, and backgrounds all follow the pixel style, maintaining visual consistency and enhancing user immersion. The background also changes automatically throughout the day, transitioning from morning to evening lighting, which further improves the visual experience.

In contrast, Intersymmetric has a more abstract and minimalist design. The page background is pure white, and the text is displayed in colorful gradient fonts. However, the site lacks a strong visual hierarchy and guidance. The styles and layouts of the interactive elements and loading buttons are inconsistent, which weakens the overall unity of the interface design.

Orb.Farm creates a visually comfortable atmosphere with rich colors and detailed content. Intersymmetric, on the other hand, uses a minimalist approach to express its ideas, but it appears weaker in terms of visual consistency for the user.

Feedback

On the Orb.Farm website, every user action receives immediate and clear visual feedback. For example, when clicking to add an element, the new creature instantly appears on the page. The feedback mechanism is effective and easy to understand from a visual perspective. I think that if sound feedback were added on top of this, the overall experience of the site would feel even more complete.

The feedback on Intersymmetric is mainly sound-based. When users interact with the graphical controls, they trigger synth-like audio changes. This type of sound feedback is artistic and improvisational, which enhances the sensory level of interaction. However, due to the lack of a clear mapping between action and response, users may find it difficult to tell whether their interaction was successful.

Orb.Farm focuses on making the result of each action clearly visible, while Intersymmetric leans toward sound-driven artistic feedback. These two sites represent different approaches—functionality versus sensory expression.
